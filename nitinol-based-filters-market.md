<p><p>Nitinol-based filters are utilized primarily in the treatment of venous thromboembolism, designed to intercept blood clots before they reach the lungs. The market is divided into two main types: permanent inferior vena cava (IVC) filters and retrievable IVC filters. Permanent IVC filters are intended for long-term placement, providing ongoing protection against embolisms. In contrast, retrievable IVC filters offer flexibility, allowing for removal once the risk of clot formation has diminished, addressing varying patient needs and preferences.</p></p>
