<p><p>The blood collection tube market comprises various types that serve distinct purposes. Serum Separating Tubes (SST) facilitate the separation of serum from blood clots, ideal for chemistry tests. EDTA Tubes contain the anticoagulant ethylenediaminetetraacetic acid, preserving cellular components for hematology analyses. Plasma Separation Tubes (PST) are designed for quick plasma separation, often used in clinical chemistry. Other market segments may include specialized tubes for specific tests or additives, catering to diverse laboratory needs and enhancing diagnostic accuracy.</p></p>

<p><p>The blood collection tube market serves two primary applications: venous and capillary blood collection. Capillary blood collection, often performed via finger-pricking or heel-pricking in infants, allows for quick and convenient sample collection, especially in point-of-care settings. Both applications require specific tube types to ensure proper sample integrity and functionality for various medical tests and procedures.</p></p>
